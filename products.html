Rose

Rose, also called (genus Rosa), genus of some 100 species of perennial shrubs in the rose family (Rosaceae). They are native primarily to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Many of the roses are cultivated for their beautiful flowers, which range in colour from white through various tones of yellow and pink to dark crimson,maroon, and most have a delightful fragrance, which varies according to the variety and different climatic conditions.


Marigold

Marigolds are one of the popular annual flowers in the whole world, easily grown from seed or transplant, and when given the right conditions will overflow with bright blossoms that fill the garden with beautiful color. They are equally suitable for containers, as they are in the garden, and are grown as a long-lasting cut flower, with a clean, crisp scent.


Lily

Lily, also known as (genus Lilium), genus of 80 to 100 species of herbaceous flowering plants of the family Liliaceae, native to temperate areas of the Northern Hemisphere. Many of them are prized as ornamental plants, and they have been extensively hybridized. The flowers has six petal-like segments, which may form the shape of a trumpet, with a more or less elongated tube.


Tulip

Tulip, other name (genus Tulipa), any of a group of cultivated bulbous herbs in the family Liliaceae. The genus Tulipa consists of about 100 species which are native to Eurasia from Austria and Italy eastward to Japan, with two-thirds of them native to the eastern Mediterranean and the southeastern parts of the Soviet Union. These tulips are among the most popular of all garden flowers. The tulip produces two or three thick, bluish green leaves that are clustered at the base of the plant.
